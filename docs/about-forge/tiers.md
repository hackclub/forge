# The Tier System

Forge projects are funded based on both the time you spent on it, as well as the quality of the project.

## Tiers

The following are just guidelines. We can't promise what tier a project you build will get before you start building, as it also depends on your implementation of it. Your final tier and coin payout will be determined by your reviewer.

### Tier 1 (7c/hour):
The best of the best!

Tier 1 projects are ambitious, polished and unique, the sort of work that could become a product or a showcase piece. 

- You must pitch Tier 1 projects in the #forgery (Slack). See [pitching](/docs/requirements/pitching)
- Major technical work should be your own: custom firmware, custom electronics, and original mechanical design


Examples:
- Custom 3D printer (personally i want to see some 4 or 5 axis printers come out of forge lol)
- amateur rockets




### Tier 2 (5.5c/hour):

Tier 2 projects are still substantial and well executed but slightly narrower in scope than Tier 1. These projects usually include multiple components (software + hardware, firmware + mechanics, or nontrivial electronics).


- There should be a significant amount of original work. You can use libraries and modules for helpers (and please credit them!), but the core functionality should be implemented by you.


Examples:
- Custom flipper zero / hacker swiss army knife
- Full split keyboard (polished!)
- Single board computer

### Tier 3 (4.5c/hour):

Tier 3 is for smaller but meaningful projects, and can be a good starting point for learning new skills with some background knowledge.


For T3 projects, it’s okay to reuse modules or libraries for non‑core pieces, but be transparent about what’s reused and what you built. That being said, there is still a distinction between you designing and building something, compared to just assembling something.



Examples:
- Custom devboard 
- Modular keyboard

### Tier 4 (4c/hour):

Tier 4 covers small experiments to get started with electronics, or to experiment with new ideas or tools.


Similar to T3 projects, it is important to distinguish building from assembling. Tutorials and guides are allowed, but we don't want to see 1:1 copies. Try to implement something unique, to make it your own!

Examples:
- Hackpad (but put your original twist on it!)
